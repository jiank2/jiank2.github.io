I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Rochester (UR). I am also affiliated with the Goergen Institute of Data Science. My research lies in data mining and machine learning for social good with applications in material science, healthcare, agriculture, earth science, smart cities, education, political science, and e-commerce. Prior to joining UR, I received my Ph.D. degree in Computer Science at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign (UIUC), where I am lucky to be advised by Dr. Hanghang Tong.

To prospective students: I am always looking for highly motivated students to join my research group or work with me as summer interns. If you are interested in working with me, please send me an email with your CV and a brief introduction of yourself (e.g., your background, past experiences, and research interests).

NOTE: Unfortunately, due to the large amount of emails, I won't be able to reply to them all.

Research

I am generally interested in data mining, machine learning and artificial intelligence. My research are mostly driven by the following research questions:
  • How can we learn the interconnected world effectively and efficiently?
  • How can we avoid the negative societal impacts of the learned model?
  • How can we facilitate the human-machine collaborations?
